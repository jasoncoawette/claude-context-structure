# Tax Deductions

## What this does

Reviews business expenses to identify what is deductible, assigns the correct tax category, and ties each deduction to supporting documentation for the relevant tax year.

## When to use

- Preparing for tax filing and reviewing the year's expenses.
- Deciding whether a specific purchase qualifies as deductible.
- Building a substantiated deductions schedule for a CPA.

## Process

1. Pull the expense list for the tax year and group by likely category.
2. Mark each expense deductible, partial, or personal; note the rule applied.
3. Attach a receipt or statement reference and total deductions by category.

## Common pitfalls

- Claiming personal or mixed-use expenses without splitting the business portion.
- Categorizing expenses without a receipt to substantiate them.
- Applying the wrong tax year's rules or limits.
