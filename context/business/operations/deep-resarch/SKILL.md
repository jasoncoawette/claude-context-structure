# Deep Research

## What this does

Breaks a business question into sub-questions, gathers evidence from multiple independent sources, verifies claims adversarially, and synthesizes a cited report with a clear recommendation.

## When to use

- A decision (market, vendor, pricing, competitor) needs more than a quick search.
- Conflicting information must be reconciled across sources.
- You need a written, cited brief others can trust.

## Process

1. Refine the question and define what a complete answer must cover.
2. Fan out searches across independent sources; capture URLs and dates.
3. Cross-check key claims, then synthesize findings with citations and a recommendation.

## Common pitfalls

- Treating a single source or AI summary as fact without corroboration.
- Letting the conclusion drift ahead of the evidence.
- Omitting citations, making the report impossible to verify later.

## Scripts

- `Script.py` — fan out queries, dedupe sources, and assemble a cited research brief.
