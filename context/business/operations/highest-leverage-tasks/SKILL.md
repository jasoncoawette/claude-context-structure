# Highest-Leverage Tasks

## What this does

Takes a list of possible tasks or goals and ranks them by leverage — impact relative to effort — so time goes to the few things that matter most instead of busywork.

## When to use

- The to-do list or backlog is too long and needs prioritizing.
- Deciding what to focus on this week or sprint.
- A goal needs to be broken into the highest-impact next actions.

## Process

1. Gather candidate tasks and tie each to a concrete business outcome.
2. Score each by impact and effort; flag anything that unblocks other work.
3. Rank, recommend the top few, and explicitly note what to drop or defer.

## Common pitfalls

- Confusing urgent-but-low-impact tasks with truly high-leverage ones.
- Ranking without honest effort estimates, so big tasks look deceptively cheap.
- Producing a list with no clear "do this first" recommendation.
